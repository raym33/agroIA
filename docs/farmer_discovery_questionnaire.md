# Farmer discovery questionnaire

Goal: understand which data already exists, how the farmer actually works today, and which daily decision is valuable enough to pay for.

## How to use it

- Run this as a 15-20 minute interview.
- Do not start by selling the product.
- Ask for one real example of an Excel file, notebook, or photo set if the farmer is willing to show it.

## Core questions

1. Which crop or crops do you manage, and how many plots do you actively work with?
2. Which decision costs you the most time or money right now: irrigation, pests, yield, labor, or something else?
3. How do you currently decide how much to irrigate and how often?
4. Do you store the data anywhere today: Excel, notebook, phone, ERP, cooperative records, or just memory?
5. If you use Excel or CSV, which columns do you actually have in real life?
6. Is your history organized by day, by week, or only by season?
7. Do you record applied water, production, observed pests, treatments, or costs?
8. Do you take crop photos? If yes, who takes them, with which device, and where are they stored?
9. Which alert would be most useful to receive on your phone before it is too late?
10. If an app told you how much to irrigate today and where to inspect pest pressure, what minimum accuracy would you need to trust it?

## Follow-up questions

- Who else uses that data besides you?
- Does your cooperative or agronomist already request a specific format?
- Which data is easy to collect, and which data is practically unavailable today?
- Which mistake is more expensive: over-irrigating, under-irrigating, or reacting too late to a pest problem?
- How often would you check an app like this: several times a day, once a day, or only when something looks wrong?

## What to observe during the interview

- Whether they think in specific plots or only at farm level
- Whether dates are consistent or mixed
- Whether plot naming is stable
- Whether applied water exists as a real measure or only as an estimate
- Whether photos can be linked to both plot and date

## Signs of a good fit

- They already use some recurring spreadsheet or field notebook
- Water or pests are real pain points
- They want a simple daily recommendation
- They can identify plots and dates with limited chaos

## Risk signs

- They store nothing and do not want to start recording
- The format changes completely every season
- They do not distinguish plots or irrigation sectors
- They expect advanced predictions without minimum data discipline

## Recommended close

At the end of the conversation, ask for one of these:

- one real spreadsheet, anonymized if needed
- 5-10 photos from a plot with approximate dates
- permission to convert their current format into a shared template
